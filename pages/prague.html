Prague

Places to go

  • Charles Bridge
  • Old Town Square
  • Prague Castle
  • Museum of Communism (there are 2, both are worth a visit)
  • John Lennon Wall

Transport

The metro system in Prague is very simple and relatively easy to understand. The main problem was, however, was that there was only a small number of stops, so we found that we would have to walk for quite a while to get to our desired destination. Nevertheless, tickets are quite cheap (as is everything else in Prague!) Prices of trains, buses and trams for adults, seniors & students: Single Ticket for 90 minutes travel - 32czk Single Ticket for 30 minutes travel - 24czk 1-Day Pass (24 hours) - 110czk 3-Day Pass (72 hours) - 310czk

Student Hacks

  • Don’t go to the largest nightclub, Karlovy lazne, because it’s very overrated (unless you are there at the weekend). Instead take a stroll up to Letna Beer Gardens overlooking the city at night (crack open a cold one with the lads/ladies).
  • Go on a free walking tour! These can be found in most European cities, but be sure to book them online at least a week in advance, especially during peak season. Prague is a great place to try this as there is so much to see and the tour guides are very knowledgeable and friendly, making the tours thoroughly enjoyable. Useful tour sites include
    • Sandeman tours
    • Freetours.com
  • Paying to rent a pedal boat for an hour is well worth the money. You’ll get to ride it around the Charles Bridge area and if you time your excursion well, you can catch a magnificent view of the sun setting over the Prague castle.
  • DO NOT pay to go the Old New Synagogue, ( supposedly the oldest synagogue in Europe) - NOT WORTH IT - either get the ticket for all of the synagogues, or just go to the Jubilee Synagogue, (the pretty one).

Rome

Places to go

  • The Vatican- St. Peter’s Basilica is free to enter
  • Spanish Steps
  • Pantheon
  • Colosseum
  • Villa Borghese

Transport

Rome was probably the busiest city we visited, compared to the others, which made getting around slightly harder. However, we found the metro and bus system easy to understand, and very reliable. Prices: Single ticket - €1.50 24h ticket - €7.00 48h ticket - €12.50 72h ticket - €18.00 7-day ticket - €24.00

Student Hacks

  • If you fancy a break from the bustling city, return train tickets to the beach can be bought for little over €5. Pack a picnic, a book and swimwear to complete your relaxation.
  • UBER IS ILLEGAL SO MAKE SURE TO GET SOMEONE WHO SPEAKS ITALIAN TO HELP YOU BOOK A TAXI OR YOU MIGHT GET STRANDED, ESPECIALLY SO IF YOU HAVE AN EARLY FLIGHT.
  • The Villa Borghese gardens are free to enter and there are always various activities going on, including a modern art museum in the garden complex that is well worth a visit. There are also beautiful views of the sunset from several lookout points as well as a replica of Shakespeare’s Globe Theatre.
